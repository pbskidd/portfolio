The Hotel Hershey

Mediterranean-inspired Luxury Meets Chocolate Heritage

Project Overview

Perched atop Pat’s Hill and opened in 1933 by Milton S. Hershey during the depths of the Great Depression, The Hotel Hershey is celebrated for its Spanish Colonial Revival architecture, chocolate-infused hospitality, and Historic Hotels of America designation. Our aim was to weave that storied legacy into a series of bespoke Ecuadane blankets—luxurious keepsakes that echo the hotel’s Mediterranean inspiration while enveloping guests in warmth and chocolate-brown tones.

The Challenge

Blend heritage and indulgence: capture the hotel’s historic elegance without compromising the playful guest experience. Blankets needed to be equally at home in suites, the award-winning spa, and the Chocolate World® retail space.

Our Approach

Working with the resort’s merchandising team, we designed a reversible throw featuring:

  • Intricate landscapes image, representing the splendor of the main building
  • A palette of colors representing Hotel Hershey’s iconic brand
  • Subtle inclusind of the 1933 founding to the side of the silhouette of the hotel façade
  • Soft, recycled high-performance weave for both sustainability and year-round Pennsylvania comfort

The Result

The blankets debuted in premium guest suites and The Circular® dining room, where they provide a cozy touch for al-fresco patio evenings. They’ve since become one of the resort’s top-selling signature items—souvenirs guests carry home as a tangible taste of Hershey’s sweetness. Staff now place a branded throw on turndown service for anniversary packages and spa retreats, reinforcing the hotel’s commitment to comfort and heritage.

“Our guests adore the blankets—each one feels like a handwritten love letter from Hershey, capturing both our history and our commitment to genuine hospitality.”

The Hotel Hershey Merchandising Team

Retail & Guest Experience Manager
